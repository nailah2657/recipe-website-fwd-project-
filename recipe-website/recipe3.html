Dal Makhani

Punjab's most iconic comfort dish — whole black lentils slow-cooked with kidney beans, butter, and cream into a luxuriously thick, smoky dal. Best made low and slow.

Prep

10 min

Cook

50 min

Serves

4

Difficulty

Easy

Spice

Mild

Calories

290

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal), soaked overnight
  • ¼ cup kidney beans (rajma), soaked overnight
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes, puréed
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Naan or roti to serve

Method

01
Pressure cook the lentils. Drain soaked lentils and kidney beans. Pressure cook with 3 cups of water and salt for 4–5 whistles until completely soft. Alternatively simmer on the stovetop for 1.5–2 hours. Partially mash with the back of a spoon.

💡 The more you mash, the creamier and thicker the dal becomes.

02
Temper the spices. Heat butter and oil in a deep pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and cook until golden, about 8 minutes.
03
Build the masala. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook 2 minutes. Add tomato purée and all spices. Cook on medium heat until the oil separates, about 10 minutes.
04
Combine and simmer. Add cooked lentils to the pan, stir to combine. Add water to adjust consistency. Simmer on low for 20–25 minutes, stirring often to prevent sticking.

💡 The longer it simmers, the deeper and richer the flavour. Restaurant dal makhani is often cooked for 8+ hours.

05
Finish with cream. Stir in heavy cream and garam masala. Cook for 5 more minutes on low until the cream is fully incorporated and the dal is luxuriously creamy.
06
Serve. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot, topped with a knob of butter, a drizzle of cream, and fresh coriander alongside warm naan.
