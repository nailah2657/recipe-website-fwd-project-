Palak Paneer

A North Indian classic — fresh spinach blended into a vibrant, velvety sauce cradling golden cubes of paneer. Nutritious, beautiful, and ready in 30 minutes.

Prep

10 min

Cook

20 min

Serves

4

Difficulty

Easy

Spice

Mild

Calories

250

Ingredients

  • 400 g fresh paneer, cubed
  • 500 g fresh spinach (palak)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chilli, slit (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ cup cream
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt to taste

Method

01
Blanch the spinach. Boil a pot of water. Add spinach and blanch for exactly 2 minutes until wilted and bright green. Immediately transfer to ice cold water to lock in the colour. Drain, then blend to a smooth purée.

💡 Don't over-blanch — keeping the vivid green makes the dish look stunning.

02
Fry the paneer. Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a pan over medium-high. Fry paneer cubes until golden on all sides, 4–5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
03
Build the masala. In the same pan, add remaining ghee. Splutter cumin seeds. Add onions and cook until golden, 6 minutes. Add ginger, garlic and chilli; cook 2 more minutes.
04
Add tomatoes and spices. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric and coriander powder. Cook until tomatoes are completely soft and the masala comes together, about 5–6 minutes. Let cool slightly, then blend this mixture smooth.
05
Combine. Return the blended tomato masala to the pan. Stir in the spinach purée. Season with salt, garam masala and a pinch of nutmeg. Simmer on low for 5 minutes.
06
Add paneer and cream. Gently fold in the fried paneer and cream. Simmer 2–3 minutes until the paneer is heated through.
07
Serve. Spoon into a serving bowl, swirl extra cream on top, and dust with a pinch of garam masala. Serve immediately with hot roti or naan.
