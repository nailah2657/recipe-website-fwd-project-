Hyderabadi Biryani

The crown jewel of Indian rice dishes — fragrant basmati layered with spiced chicken, golden fried onions, fresh mint, and a whisper of saffron. The dum method seals every grain with flavour.

Prep

40 min

Cook

60 min

Serves

6

Difficulty

Hard

Spice

Medium–Hot

Calories

520

Ingredients

  • 1 kg bone-in chicken pieces
  • 500 g basmati rice, soaked 30 min
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup plain yoghurt
  • 4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves
  • ¼ cup fresh coriander
  • Pinch of saffron in 3 tbsp warm milk
  • 4 cardamom pods, 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt to taste

Method

01
Marinate the chicken. Combine chicken with yoghurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, turmeric, chilli powder, garam masala, lemon juice and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour — overnight is ideal.
02
Fry the onions. Heat ghee in a deep heavy pot. Fry sliced onions on medium-low heat, stirring often, for 20–25 minutes until deep golden and crispy. Remove half for garnishing.

💡 Patience here is essential — properly fried onions are the backbone of authentic biryani flavour.

03
Cook the chicken. In the same pot with remaining onions and ghee, add marinated chicken. Cook on medium-high for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the masala dries and oil separates.
04
Par-cook the rice. Boil a large pot of salted water with cardamom, bay leaves and cloves. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done — it should still have a bite. Drain and set aside.
05
Layer the biryani. Over the chicken, spread half the par-cooked rice. Scatter mint, coriander and half the saffron milk. Add remaining rice, then drizzle with remaining saffron milk, reserved fried onions and a spoon of ghee.
06
Dum cook. Seal the pot tightly with foil, then cover with the lid. Cook on high 5 minutes, then on the lowest flame for 25 minutes. This steam technique — called dum — infuses every grain.

💡 Place the pot on a flat tawa over the flame to protect the bottom layer from burning.

07
Serve. Gently mix from the bottom to combine layers. Garnish with crispy onions and serve alongside raita.
