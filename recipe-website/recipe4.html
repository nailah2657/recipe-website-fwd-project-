Gulab Jamun

India's most celebrated sweet — soft milk-solid dumplings fried golden and soaked in fragrant rose and cardamom syrup. A staple of every festive table.

Prep

20 min

Cook

25 min

Serves

8

Difficulty

Medium

Spice

None

Calories

210

Ingredients

  • Dough
  • 1 cup full-fat milk powder
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee or unsalted butter
  • 3–4 tbsp warm milk (to bind)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Syrup
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1½ cups water
  • 4 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • Few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Method

01
Make the syrup. Combine sugar, water, cardamom and lemon juice in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, stirring until dissolved. Reduce heat and simmer 5–6 minutes until lightly sticky. Add rose water and saffron. Keep warm on the lowest heat.

💡 The syrup must be warm — not boiling — when you add the fried jamuns so they absorb it properly.

02
Mix the dough. Combine milk powder, flour and baking soda. Rub in ghee until it resembles fine breadcrumbs. Add warm milk one tablespoon at a time, gently kneading to form a soft, smooth dough. Do not over-knead. Rest 5 minutes.
03
Shape the jamuns. Divide dough into 20–24 equal portions. Roll each between your palms into a perfectly smooth ball — any surface crack will cause it to break during frying.

💡 Dampen your palms slightly if the balls are cracking as you roll.

04
Fry slowly. Heat oil on medium-low (around 150°C). Fry the dough balls, stirring gently and continuously, for 8–10 minutes until deep golden brown all over.

💡 Low and slow frying ensures they cook through to the centre without burning outside.

05
Soak in syrup. Drain on paper towels for 1–2 minutes, then slide into the warm syrup. Soak for at least 30 minutes — they will puff up as they absorb the syrup.
06
Serve. Serve warm or at room temperature in a bowl with generous syrup. Garnish with chopped pistachios and dried rose petals. Divine alongside vanilla ice cream.
