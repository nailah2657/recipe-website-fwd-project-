Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

India's most beloved curry — tender chicken marinated in yoghurt and spices, simmered in a silky tomato-cream sauce. Born in Delhi in the 1950s, loved everywhere since.

Prep

20 min

Cook

35 min

Serves

4

Difficulty

Medium

Spice

Mild–Medium

Calories

380

Ingredients

  • 700 g boneless chicken thighs, cubed
  • 1 cup plain yoghurt
  • 2 tsp tandoori masala
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp butter + 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 400 g crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 150 ml heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander & naan to serve

Method

01
Marinate the chicken. Mix yoghurt, tandoori masala, chilli powder and salt. Coat the chicken cubes well, cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes — overnight gives the best results.

💡 The longer you marinate, the more tender and flavourful the chicken.

02
Sear the chicken. Heat oil in a pan over high heat. Sear the marinated chicken in batches until charred outside and cooked through, about 6–8 minutes. Set aside.
03
Build the sauce. In the same pan, melt butter over medium heat. Sauté onions until golden, 8 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, cook 2 more minutes until fragrant.
04
Add spices and tomatoes. Stir in coriander powder, cumin and chilli powder; cook 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes and sugar. Simmer 15 minutes until oil starts to separate.
05
Blend smooth. Let the sauce cool slightly, then blend until completely smooth. Return to the pan over low heat.

💡 Pass through a sieve for an ultra-silky, restaurant-style sauce.

06
Finish with cream. Stir in cream and garam masala. Add the seared chicken and simmer 8–10 minutes until the chicken absorbs all the flavours.
07
Serve. Taste and adjust salt. Ladle into bowls, swirl extra cream on top and finish with fresh coriander. Serve with warm naan or basmati rice.
